# GlyLens Corpus Gap Analysis v1.0
_Last Updated: 2026-06-20_

This analysis reviews the current GlyLens corpus package and identifies missing coverage across:
- Ingredient corpus
- Food corpus
- Product corpus
- India pack
- US pack
- Benchmark dataset

It categorizes gaps by severity and then prioritizes additions for M1 using a 50/50/50 expansion strategy.

## 1. Summary

The existing corpus has strong foundational coverage for diabetes-relevant grains, legumes, vegetables, proteins, and a handful of dairy and packaged foods. Major gaps remain in:
- low-GI fruits and beverages
- restaurant and prepared meal coverage
- Indian regional staples beyond south Indian and wheat-based meals
- US retail/restaurant staples and packaged diabetic-marketing products
- benchmark anchors for beverages, fast-casual meals, and Indian branded products

This gap analysis identifies the largest shortfalls and proposes a prioritized M1 expansion.

## 2. Gap Categories

### 2.1 Critical Gaps

1. Missing diabetic-relevant low-GI fruits: berries, kiwi, pomegranate, stone fruit, and citrus variety.
2. Missing packaged diabetic/low-GI beverage products: unsweetened almond milk, fortified soy milk, green tea powder, and sugar-free sports drinks.
3. Missing restaurant/prepared meals for Indian and US consumers: bowl meals, restaurant curries, salads, dosa specialty variants, and fast-casual grain bowls.
4. Missing benchmark items for beverages and branded packaged products with measured GI/GL.
5. Missing Indian regional staples from western, eastern, and north-eastern cuisines.

### 2.2 High Gaps

1. Missing US staple grain and protein meals such as whole-grain sandwich, burrito bowl, turkey chili, and bean burger.
2. Missing packaged snack foods marketed for diabetes or metabolic health.
3. Missing beverages beyond milk-based smoothies, especially fermented drinks, buttermilk, coffee/tea profiles, and coconut water.
4. Missing fruit and vegetable variety in both India and US packs.
5. Missing benchmark coverage of common restaurant/ready-to-eat items.

### 2.3 Medium Gaps

1. Missing ingredient-level seeds and nuts such as walnuts, pistachios, pumpkin seeds, and sunflower seeds.
2. Missing Indian pulses and dals such as toor dal, masoor dal, horse gram, and fresh green peas.
3. Missing US cereal alternatives like whole-grain corn, buckwheat noodles, and fortified breakfast mixes.
4. Missing packaged Indian meal kits and US refrigerated healthy bowls.
5. Missing benchmark anchors for mixed meals with protein and vegetables.

### 2.4 Low Gaps

1. Missing some discretionary or regional seasonings like curry leaf, asafoetida, and Indian spice blends.
2. Missing lower-priority convenience products such as low-fat cheese slices and plant-based snack bars.
3. Missing some cross-market items that are useful but not essential for diabetes signal quality.

### 3.6 Benchmark Dataset

Critical benchmark gaps:
- No beverage benchmarks for common diabetic-friendly drinks.
- No restaurant/ready-to-eat meal benchmarks with measured GI/GL.
- No Indian branded packaged product benchmarks beyond a few staples.
- No US fast-casual bowl or salad benchmark.

High benchmark gaps:
- Missing benchmark items for seeds and nuts with strong glycemic signals.
- Missing benchmark foods for mixed-meal breakfast products.
- Missing benchmark coverage for plant-based dairy alternatives.

Medium benchmark gaps:
- No dedicated benchmark for fermented drinks such as buttermilk, kombucha, or low-sugar yogurt drinks.
- No benchmark entries for packaged snack bars marketed to diabetics.

Low benchmark gaps:
- No benchmark for some spice blends and seasoning ingredients.

## 5. Recommended Actions

1. Add the critical and high priority items to the India and US packs immediately.
2. Expand benchmark seed data with beverages, restaurant bowls, diabetic-marketed products, and regional Indian staples.
3. Treat restaurant and packaged product coverage as a separate sub-corpus in M1 to ensure meal-level insight.
4. Label every new item with evidence source, GI/GL status, and market region.

## 6. Document Updates

This gap analysis should be included in the master documentation index and referenced by the corpus build package as the operational roadmap for the M1 expansion.
